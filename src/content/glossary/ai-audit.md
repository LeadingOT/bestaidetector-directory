# What is AI Audit

**An AI Audit is a systematic, independent examination of an artificial intelligence system to assess its fairness, accuracy, security, compliance, and overall impact.**

An AI audit evaluates the entire AI lifecycle, from the data used to train a model to its real-world deployment and outcomes. Unlike a traditional financial audit, it is a multidisciplinary process involving data scientists, ethicists, legal experts, and domain specialists. The core objective is to provide stakeholders with verifiable evidence that an AI system operates as intended, aligns with stated ethical principles, and complies with relevant regulations and standards. This involves technical assessments (e.g., checking for algorithmic bias, model accuracy, and robustness) as well as process reviews (e.g., examining data governance, development protocols, and monitoring practices).

The need for AI audits has grown alongside regulatory frameworks like the EU's AI Act and sector-specific guidelines. They serve as a critical risk management tool, helping organizations identify and mitigate potential harms such as discriminatory decisions, privacy violations, security vulnerabilities, or operational failures. An audit report typically details findings, identifies gaps, and provides actionable recommendations for remediation, creating a roadmap for responsible AI governance.

**Why it matters / Use cases:**
AI audits are essential for building trust and ensuring accountability. For a bank using an AI model to approve loans, an audit can detect if the model unfairly denies applicants from a particular demographic. A healthcare provider using diagnostic AI would audit for clinical accuracy and data privacy compliance. Companies also conduct audits proactively to validate their AI's performance before launch, to meet contractual obligations with clients, or to certify their systems against industry standards. Ultimately, an AI audit helps organizations deploy AI confidently, protect their reputation, and demonstrate due diligence to regulators, customers, and the public.
