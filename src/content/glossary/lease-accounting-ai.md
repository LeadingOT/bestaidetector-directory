# What is Lease Accounting AI

**Lease Accounting AI refers to the application of artificial intelligence, specifically machine learning and natural language processing, to automate and enhance the processes involved in identifying, extracting, and managing lease data for financial reporting under standards like ASC 842 and IFRS 16.**

Lease Accounting AI automates the most manual and error-prone stages of lease compliance. Its core function is to read and interpret lease contracts and related documents (e.g., PDFs, scanned images, emails). Using technologies like Optical Character Recognition (OCR) and Natural Language Processing (NLP), the software can automatically locate key data points—such as lease start/end dates, payment amounts, renewal options, and discount rates—without manual data entry. This extracted data is then validated, structured, and fed directly into a lease accounting engine to calculate the Right-of-Use (ROU) asset and lease liability, generate journal entries, and maintain the required lease schedules.

This technology is crucial because modern lease accounting standards require all leases (with some exceptions) to be recognized on the balance sheet. Organizations may have hundreds or thousands of leases embedded across various departments and document formats, making manual compilation a massive, costly, and risky undertaking. AI mitigates this by providing speed, scale, and accuracy. For example, a retail chain with 500 store leases can use AI to process all contracts in days instead of months, ensuring a consistent and auditable data extraction process that reduces the risk of human error in identifying critical clauses.

**Why it Matters / Use Cases:** Lease Accounting AI is essential for achieving efficient, accurate, and audit-ready compliance. Key use cases include: initial adoption projects for ASC 842/IFRS 16, where historical leases must be quickly onboarded; ongoing lease portfolio management, where new or modified contracts are automatically processed; and merger & acquisition due diligence, where the target company's lease obligations must be rapidly assessed. It transforms lease accounting from a reactive, compliance-heavy task into a strategic, data-driven function, freeing accounting teams to focus on analysis and exception management rather than data mining.

**Related Terms:** ASC 842, IFRS 16, Right-of-Use (ROU) Asset, Lease Liability, Natural Language Processing (NLP), Optical Character Recognition (OCR), Lease Management Software.
