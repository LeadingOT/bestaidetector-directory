# What is Financial Close Automation

**Financial Close Automation (FCA) is the use of software to streamline, standardize, and accelerate the repetitive tasks involved in closing a company's accounting books at the end of a reporting period.**

The financial close, or "closing the books," is a critical accounting cycle where an organization finalizes its financial statements for a period (month, quarter, or year). Traditionally, this process is manual, involving collecting data from disparate systems, reconciling accounts, preparing journal entries, and compiling reports. Financial Close Automation replaces these manual, spreadsheet-heavy workflows with dedicated software. This technology centralizes data, enforces consistent procedures through checklists and workflows, and automates the execution of key tasks.

For example, instead of an accountant manually downloading bank statements and comparing them line-by-line to the general ledger in Excel, FCA software can automatically import bank feeds, match transactions using rules, and flag exceptions for review. Similarly, intercompany reconciliations—a common bottleneck—can be automated to identify and resolve mismatches between subsidiary entities instantly. The software provides a centralized dashboard, giving controllers and CFOs real-time visibility into the close's status, with a clear audit trail of every action taken.

This automation matters because it directly addresses the core challenges of the manual close: it is error-prone, time-consuming, and lacks visibility. By automating repetitive tasks, FCA reduces the risk of human error, improves compliance, and can shorten the close timeline from days to hours. This allows finance teams to shift from being data collectors and processors to becoming analytical advisors who can provide strategic insights. Key use cases include automating bank and balance sheet reconciliations, managing journal entry workflows and approvals, consolidating financial data from multiple entities, and ensuring task management and compliance for controls like SOX.
