# What is OCR in Accounting

**OCR (Optical Character Recognition) in accounting is the technology that automatically extracts text and data from scanned documents or images, such as invoices and receipts, and converts it into machine-readable, searchable, and editable digital data.**

At its core, OCR software analyzes the shapes and patterns of characters in an image file (like a PDF scan or a smartphone photo) and translates them into actual text characters a computer can understand. In modern accounting, this process is often enhanced by Intelligent Document Processing (IDP) or AI, which goes beyond simple character recognition. These advanced systems can identify document types, understand the context of data (like distinguishing an invoice number from a date), and accurately map extracted information to the correct fields in an accounting system.

This technology directly automates the traditionally manual and error-prone task of data entry. Instead of an employee manually keying in details from a stack of paper invoices, OCR software can process them in batches. For example, when a digital image of a supplier's invoice is processed, the OCR tool can extract the vendor name, invoice number, date, line item details, and total amount due with high accuracy.

OCR matters because it is a foundational technology for accounting automation, driving significant gains in efficiency, accuracy, and cost reduction. It eliminates manual data entry errors, accelerates the accounts payable and receivable cycles, and allows financial staff to focus on higher-value tasks like analysis and exception handling. Key use cases include automated invoice processing, digitizing and organizing historical paper records, streamlining expense report management by reading receipts, and automating bank reconciliation by capturing data from statements.

**Related terms:** **Intelligent Document Processing (IDP)**, **Accounts Payable (AP) Automation**, **Data Capture**, **Machine Learning**, **Digital Transformation**.
