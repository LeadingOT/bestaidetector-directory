# What is Expense Categorization

**Expense categorization is the systematic process of classifying business or personal financial transactions into specific, predefined accounting categories for the purpose of tracking, reporting, and analyzing spending.**

At its core, expense categorization transforms raw transaction data—like a credit card charge for "Office Depot"—into structured financial information. This is done by assigning each expense to a logical category, such as "Office Supplies," "Software Subscriptions," "Travel & Entertainment," or "Cost of Goods Sold." Modern accounting and financial software often automates this process using rules, machine learning, and merchant code data, though manual review and adjustment are frequently necessary for accuracy.

The detailed explanation involves understanding its role in bookkeeping. Categorization is the critical step that enables the organization of the general ledger, the core record of a company's financial transactions. Accurate categorization ensures expenses are recorded against the correct accounts, which is non-negotiable for producing reliable financial statements like the Profit & Loss (P&L) statement. For example, correctly distinguishing between a "Capital Expense" (like a new laptop, an asset) and an "Operational Expense" (like monthly utility bills) has significant tax and accounting implications.

This process matters because it directly informs financial health, strategic decision-making, and regulatory compliance. For a small business owner, categorizing expenses reveals exactly where money is being spent, identifying areas for potential cost-saving. For accountants, it is essential for preparing accurate tax returns, ensuring deductible expenses are properly claimed. Use cases include budgeting (comparing planned vs. actual spending in each category), financial reporting for investors, and simplifying audits by having a clear, organized trail of transactions.

**Related terms:** Chart of Accounts, General Ledger, Bookkeeping, Profit and Loss (P&L) Statement, Receipt Capture.
