# What is AR Automation

**AR Automation is the use of software to streamline and perform the repetitive, manual tasks associated with the accounts receivable (AR) process, from invoice delivery to cash collection.**

At its core, AR Automation replaces paper-based, email-driven, and spreadsheet-reliant workflows with digital systems. This typically involves software that integrates with a company's ERP or accounting system to automatically generate and send invoices via email or customer portals immediately upon fulfillment of a service or shipment of goods. Beyond delivery, these systems automate follow-up by sending scheduled payment reminders and late notices, reconciling incoming payments (including partial payments and deductions), and providing real-time dashboards on cash flow, Days Sales Outstanding (DSO), and collector performance.

The technology matters because manual AR processes are slow, error-prone, and inefficient. They delay cash flow, create poor customer experiences, and consume significant staff time that could be spent on analysis or resolving complex disputes. By automating these tasks, businesses accelerate cash conversion, improve accuracy, enhance customer satisfaction with clear, professional communication, and gain actionable insights into their financial health. For example, a distribution company might use AR automation to instantly email invoices with a "Pay Now" link, automatically apply daily bank deposits to open invoices, and flag any account that is 15 days past due for immediate collector attention.

**Use cases** include businesses with high invoice volumes (e.g., B2B wholesalers, SaaS subscriptions, professional services), companies looking to reduce their DSO to improve working capital, and finance teams aiming to eliminate manual data entry and reduce clerical errors.

**Related terms:** **Accounts Receivable (AR)**, **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)**, **Cash Flow Management**, **Electronic Invoicing (e-Invoicing)**, **Payment Gateway**.
