# What is Automated Reconciliation

**Automated reconciliation is the use of specialized software to automatically compare two sets of financial records, identify discrepancies (differences), and resolve them or flag them for human review.**

This process replaces the traditional, manual method where accountants would painstakingly compare statements—such as bank statements, credit card feeds, or internal ledgers—against company records line by line. The software is programmed with specific rules and logic to match transactions based on criteria like date, amount, invoice number, or reference ID. When a perfect match is found, the transaction is marked as reconciled. Non-matching items are highlighted as exceptions, allowing finance teams to focus their investigative efforts only on these discrepancies, rather than on every single transaction.

For example, a business might use automated reconciliation to match hundreds of daily sales transactions from its point-of-sale (POS) system with the corresponding deposits in its bank account. The tool would automatically pair each deposit with the correct batch of sales, significantly reducing the time spent and human error inherent in manual matching. Another common application is in accounts payable, where the software matches supplier invoices received, purchase orders issued, and goods receipt notes to ensure that only verified and accurate bills are paid.

Automated reconciliation matters because it directly enhances the accuracy, efficiency, and control of the financial close process. It drastically reduces the time required for month-end closing, minimizes human error, and improves fraud detection by consistently applying rules to every transaction. This allows accounting teams to shift from repetitive data-matching tasks to more strategic analysis, such as investigating the root causes of frequent discrepancies or improving cash flow management. For growing businesses with high transaction volumes, it is essential for maintaining reliable financial records without proportionally increasing headcount.

**Related terms:** General Ledger, Bank Reconciliation, Transaction Matching, Financial Close, Exception Reporting.
